Hi, I'm Jiamin Lim.

Hi, my name is Jiamin Lim. Aspiring software developer, currently an economist based in Sydney, Australia. I am looking forward to joining the winter cohort for Founders and Coders!

I want to transition to software development as I enjoy the puzzle-solving and creative aspects of it. Strangely enough, looking back, I have been involved in coding in one way or another over the past few years. The evolution of my coding journey can be found below:

  • 2006: My first coding experience was with my personal blog in high school, where I played around with HTML and CSS to further customise some of the templates.
  • 2013-15: While studying economics at university, I self-learnt R to complete my econometrics assignments. Later on, I used R to conduct data analysis working as an economist at the Reserve Bank of Australia.
  • 2016: I took a part-time course on Data Science at General Assembly, where I used Python to implement machine learning techniques to predict the success of crowdfunding campaigns for microfinance loans (for more info, see my Github).
  • 2017-18: Realising that I was more interested in using coding as a means to create products rather than analyse data, as a fun little side project, I followed a youtube tutorial and built a simple meme creator app in Java using Android Studio. It was really fun!
  • May 2018: I found out about a free Amazon Alexa Dev day event and learnt the 101 on how to implement a voice skill. This made me very curious about what was going on under the hood and also all the fun voice apps you could make with it!
  • June 2018: I then took part in Random Hacks of Kindness, a hackathon where teams come together to help non-for-profits build tech solutions. My team partnered with a charity that works with its volunteer network to provide "free Uber for seniors". We worked closely with the charity to design and build a web-based platform that allowed volunteer drivers to view all available ride requests and sort based on criteria such as time or location. While I was mostly involved in the design and testing, it was very fascinating to see the the front-end and back-end developers working together to bring the project to life!
  • July-August 2018: I found out about Founders and Coders on a medium blog and was really excited about the peer-led learning model and social purpose aspect to it. Finally managed to finish the prereqs and apply for the winter cohort!
  • September 2018: To be continued...
